#  ➡️ Healthcare Analytics Dashboard 


##  Overview

This project presents a **Healthcare Analytics Dashboard** built using **Power BI**, designed for hospital administrators, healthcare analysts, and data enthusiasts to monitor **patient trends and financial performance in one place**.

It provides a clear view of key healthcare metrics, enabling **data-driven decision-making** for operational and financial efficiency.

##  Objectives

* Track patient and billing data efficiently
* Identify high-revenue medical conditions
* Analyze billing distribution across hospitals, doctors, and insurance providers
* Understand demographic patterns in healthcare data

##  Key Metrics

* **Total Patients:** 55.5K
* **Total Billing:** $1.42B
* **Average Billing:** $25.54K
* **Average Age:** 51.54 years
* **Average Billing per Condition:** $236.24M

##  Dashboard Features

* 📅 Monthly Billing Trends
* 🏥 Patients by Medical Condition
* 💳 Billing by Insurance Provider
* 👨‍⚕️ Top 10 Doctors by Billing
* 🏢 Top Hospitals by Revenue
* 📊 Age vs Billing (Scatter Analysis)
* 👥 Gender Distribution
* 🚑 Admission Type Analysis

##  Problem Statements

* How can hospitals track billing and patient data more efficiently in real time?
* Which medical conditions contribute the most to hospital revenue?
* What is the billing distribution across insurance providers and hospitals?
* How can management identify top-performing doctors and hospitals?
* Is there any correlation between patient demographics (age, gender) and billing?

##  Key Insights

* **Arthritis** is the most common condition with the highest patient count and billing.
* Average billing per condition is significantly high (**$236M**), indicating strong variation across diseases.
* Gender distribution is nearly balanced (~50% male & female).
* **Cigna** leads in total billing among insurance providers.
* **Top-performing doctors** contribute significantly to revenue generation.
* Billing trends remained stable from **2020–2023**, with a decline in 2024.
* Admission types are evenly distributed, with slightly higher urgent cases among females.
* Revenue is concentrated among top hospitals like **Johnson PLC** and **Smith PLC**.

##  Tools & Technologies
